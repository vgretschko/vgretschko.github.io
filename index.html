About

Profile

I am Professor of Economics at the University of Münster and a ZEW Research Associate. My research addresses how to design effective institutions when classical informational assumptions and perfect commitment fail.

After studying mathematics at the University of Münster, I completed my PhD in economics at the University of Cologne. Before joining Münster, I was Professor of Market Design at the University of Mannheim and headed the Market Design research department at ZEW.

Research

Research Agenda

My research addresses how to design effective institutions when classical informational assumptions and perfect commitment fail. I develop theoretical frameworks for dynamic mechanism design where agents gradually acquire private information, and analyze contracting under persistent renegotiation threats.

I complement theory with experimental and behavioral methods. Recent work with professional procurement managers demonstrates how unstructured communication—typically absent from standard models—systematically affects market outcomes, leading to substantially higher prices. This combination of rigorous theory with empirical and experimental evidence provides a realistic foundation for understanding and improving market institutions.

Dynamic Mechanism Design

Developing theoretical frameworks for settings where agents gradually acquire private information and classical commitment assumptions fail.

Contracting & Renegotiation

Analyzing optimal contracts under persistent renegotiation threats and developing stability concepts for real-world contracting environments.

Experimental Market Design

Experimental studies on communication in negotiations and how behavioral factors affect market outcomes in auctions and procurement.

Teaching

Courses & Innovation

Courses

I regularly teach introductory microeconomics to approximately 800 students from business administration and economics. My teaching consistently receives very good evaluations.

Innovative Formats

I have developed innovative teaching formats including a seminar where students design experiments implemented with AI systems as agents. I integrate theory and practice through case studies from policy projects, systematically strengthening students' problem-solving and discussion skills.

AI Negotiation Simulation

Together with TWS Partners AG, I have developed an AI-based negotiation simulation system used in projects with DAX-listed companies and regulatory authorities. This provides rich teaching material for contract design, auctions, and negotiation strategies.
