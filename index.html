About

Profile

I am an economist specializing in market design, auction theory, and energy economics. My research develops, simulates, and experimentally tests rules for stable and functional markets and institutions, with the goal of carrying scientific knowledge into practice.

After studying mathematics at the University of Münster, I completed my PhD in economics at the University of Cologne. Before joining the University of Münster, I was Professor of Market Design at the University of Mannheim and headed the Market Design research department at ZEW - Leibniz Centre for European Economic Research.

Research

Research Areas

Energy Market Design

Designing incentives for the expansion of renewable energies and developing market rules for local, regional, national, and European energy markets to support the energy transition.

Auction Theory & Mechanism Design

Theoretical and experimental analysis of auction mechanisms, including first-price auctions, information acquisition in auctions, and dynamic mechanisms with covert information.

Procurement Design

Studying optimal procurement mechanisms, comparing auctions vs. negotiations, and developing rules to prevent discrimination and favoritism in public and private procurement.
